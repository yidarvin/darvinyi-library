This is a library of distilled non-fiction. Each page takes one educational book and
rebuilds its ideas in original prose and original diagrams, so you can leave
understanding the core argument, the central framework, and how to use it, without
having read a line of the original. It is a synthesis, not a summary of chapters, and
never a copy.

## Ideas, not text

The line we hold is simple. Ideas are free; expression is not. We distill the concepts
and the lessons, and we write every page from scratch. There is no verbatim quoting
beyond the rare short attributed epigraph, and no close paraphrase either: swapping
synonyms into an author's sentence is still borrowing their sentence. The test we use is
plain. If we could not explain an idea with the book closed, we have not distilled it
yet.

The same rule governs the diagrams, which are the point of this site rather than its
decoration. When a book has a famous figure, we do not trace it. We build an original
visual of the underlying idea in a fixed house vocabulary, so the whole shelf reads in
one voice and no page reproduces someone else's chart.

## The shape under the words

A good non-fiction book is usually one clear idea carried by a large body of story and
evidence. Most of the page count is the carrying. A distillation keeps the shape and
lets the supporting mass go, because you can reconstruct the examples for yourself once
you hold the structure.

## Organized by our own logic

Pages are grouped on shelves by subject, not by any book's table of contents. A
distillation that mirrors the chapter order reads as a replacement for the book. One
that reorganizes the ideas by how they actually relate reads as synthesis, which is the
honest thing to make and also the more useful one. Where two books argue about the same
idea, their pages link to each other, so the library slowly becomes a light web instead
of a flat list.

## One book at a time

The library is built from a queue. Each book is researched, distilled, drawn, and
shelved in a single focused pass, then checked before it ships. Because the whole queue
is visible from the first day, you can see the full shape of the shelves right now. The
covers that look dimmed are still in the stacks, waiting their turn.

## If you want the book, buy it

A distillation is a map, not the territory. It will orient you fast and help you decide
what deserves your full attention, but it cannot replace the hours an author spent
making an idea land. Every page links out to a bookseller for that reason. If a page
makes you want the real thing, the library has done its job: go and buy it.
